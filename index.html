It started with curiosity — wanting to know how the things on a screen actually worked underneath. That pull toward "how does this work?" turned into learning to code, then into a Computer Science degree focused on AI & Machine Learning, and eventually into a career building real backend systems for real companies.

Today that curiosity shows up as a habit: every new LLM provider, every new deployment pattern, every interesting open-source tool — I want to understand it well enough to use it in production, not just read about it. That's how a CRM ended up with a self-hosted model running on RunPod, and how a customer support assistant ended up speaking three channels through one RAG pipeline.

Outside of work, I'm usually reading about whatever's new in tech — new models, new frameworks, new ways to deploy things faster — or traveling when I get the chance, because a change of scenery is the best debugger for a tired brain.
